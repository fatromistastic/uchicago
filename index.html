1. Beyond what is visible, is what is (2025)

~4.5 mins — Orchestra

Score (PDF) · Recording

This work emerges from an ongoing textual experiment: a digital corpus of ‘visionary and prophetic’ texts spanning millennia and cultures — from the Babylonian Astronomical Diaries and the Book of Traversing Eternity to Nostradamus and Blake. I trained a rudimentary large language model (LLM) on this corpus to become a kind of Post-Information-Age oracle, exploring how a definitionally ‘superhuman’ system might refract the profoundly human impulse toward prophecy. Part of a larger chresmology (a collection of prophecies) for orchestra, each movement responds to the model’s first utterances. This movement further excavates the cultural past through the collision of heavily transformed musical fragments: Barber, Messiaen, Mahler, and even transcriptions of wind chimes haunt its surface.

2. The stone and the dream are made from the same substance (2023)

~7 mins — for 2 pianos and 2 percussion

Score (PDF)

Listen on SoundCloud

This piece traces a persistent interest in my creative work—moiré patterns, self-similarity, and their musical analogues. It is also the site of a theoretical experiment, abandoned for artistic reasons. With the first harmonic gesture, I initially sought to define scales that were transformationally and inversionally equivalent to it, using voice leading and set theory to ensure a rigid harmonic consistency. The result was a banal and homogeneous harmonic world—static in a way that was unappealing to me—so, having painstakingly mapped out the harmonic plan, I then threw it away and instead sat at the piano, trying to recreate it from memory, allowing my fingers and mind to introduce errors and inconsistencies and expanding the scalar gestures in this intuitive way instead carefully balancing direction and range while focusing on defining my own harmonic consistency rather than relying on a literal mechanism for this.

The title is a quote from Scriabin’s notebooks that strikes me not just for its balanced opposites wedded in a surprising unity, but also for its appeal to human imagination in the creation of reality—our sense of the apparently solid rock is created by the same apparatus, the human subject, that creates the most ephemeral human experience: dreams.

3. Trinomial Overshot (2025)

~3 mins — Fixed media / electronic [NO SCORE]

Listen on SoundCloud

Just as the previous work played with the idea of what constitutes “the grid” rhythmically—and of existing within it while preserving the possibility of leaving it—Trinomial Overshot 11 seeks to forge a new and distinct relationship to “the grid” and to periodicity itself. Inspired by the unusual weaving patterns devised by American weaver Ada Dietz, I wrote an algorithm combining OpenMusic and Common Lisp that generates and allows me to audition stochastic, hierarchical patterns of irregular pulse duration. This piece is built entirely around five such rhythms, each of eleven pulses, colliding with additional aleatoric elements. The title, Trinomial Overshot, refers to Ada Dietz’s own beautiful tapestry of the same name.
